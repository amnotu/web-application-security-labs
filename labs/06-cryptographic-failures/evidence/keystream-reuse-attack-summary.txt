=== Keystream Reuse Attack Summary ===

Program:
Weak credit card protection simulator

Student:
Mhd Khalil Arabieh

Observed behavior:
- The program issues random-looking credit card numbers.
- The database dump shows encrypted-looking 16-character hexadecimal values.
- The key check requires 16 hexadecimal characters.
- Supplying an incorrect key fails.
- Supplying the recovered keystream decrypts the database.

Method:
One issued card number was used as known plaintext.
The matching encrypted database row was used as ciphertext.
The plaintext card number was encoded as BCD-style hexadecimal.
The keystream was recovered with XOR:

known_plaintext_bcd XOR matching_ciphertext = keystream

Recovered keystream:
0627d828616f1f07

Validation:
The recovered keystream was accepted by the program's key check.
After entering the recovered keystream, the program displayed:
"Success!! Congratulations!"

Security explanation:
The same keystream was reused to protect multiple database entries.
With one known plaintext and its matching ciphertext, the keystream can be recovered.
Once the keystream is known, the remaining protected database entries can be decrypted.

Risk:
Reusing a stream-cipher keystream breaks confidentiality. The protection fails if an attacker can obtain one plaintext/ciphertext pair.

Public evidence note:
The full decrypted card list is intentionally not included in this repository because the values resemble payment card numbers, even though they are lab-generated.
